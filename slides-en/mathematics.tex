\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Typesetting mathematics}
Inlined equations are written inside \verb|$$| characters:\\
E.g., \verb|$\sum_{k=1}^\infty a_k$| produces $\sum_{k=1}^\infty a_k$.\medskip

Equations on their own line are put into a separate
environment
\verb|\begin{equation}..\end{equation}|\footnote{This produces a number.},
\begin{equation}
\sum_{k=1}^\infty a_k,
\end{equation}
or \verb|\[..\]|\footnote{This doesn't.},
\[ \sum_{k=1}^\infty a_k. \] 
\end{frame}


\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Typesetting mathematics\dots}
Observe the differences in style when writing the equations within the text, or
separately (e.g. the limits of the sum were written differently)

\bigskip
The style can also be forced with the commands,
\begin{itemize}
\item \verb|\textstyle|
\item \verb|\displaystyle|
\end{itemize}
if needed.
\end{frame}


\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Typesetting mathematics\dots}
In the math mode, letters (the variables or constants) are typeset with slanted
typeface and extra spaces are ignored:
\begin{verbatim}
\begin{equation}
I(x) := a_1^2       b_{1,2}   c     x    .
\end{equation}
\end{verbatim}
\begin{equation}
I(x) := a_1^2       b_{1,2}   c     x    .
\end{equation}
\begin{flushleft}
If the equations are part ot the sentence,  commas should be written such that
the formulas become a natural part of the sentence.
\end{flushleft}
\end{frame}


\begin{frame}[fragile]\frametitle{Typesetting mathematics\dots}
The characters 
\begin{verbatim}
# $ % & ~ _ ^ \ { }
\end{verbatim}
have a special meaning in \LaTeX{}. If they are needed in the equations, 
one must write
\begin{verbatim}
\#   \$   \%   \&   \_   \{   \}
\end{verbatim}
For example,
\begin{verbatim}
\[ \mathcal{L}\{f\}(s) := 
   \int_0^\infty f(t) e^{-st} dt \]
\end{verbatim}
\[ \mathcal{L}\{f\}(s) := 
   \int_0^\infty f(t) e^{-st} dt \]
\end{frame}
